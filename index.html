Culture & Demographics

Kolkata is renowned for its vibrant cultural heritage and diverse demographic landscape. Bengali is the primary language, with Hindi and English also widely spoken. The city is a melting pot of traditions and modern influences, where religious harmony and a bustling arts scene go hand in hand.

Key highlights include:

  • Festivals such as Durga Puja celebrated with grandeur.
  • Traditional culinary delights like Roshogolla and Mishti Doi.
  • A diverse population with a rich mix of local traditions and modern lifestyles.

Fun Facts

  • Literary Heritage: Known as the "City of Furious Creative Energy", Kolkata is the birthplace of renowned poet Rabindranath Tagore.
  • Architectural Marvels: Home to the iconic Howrah Bridge, one of the longest cantilever bridges in the world.
  • Historic Tram System: Kolkata boasts Asia’s oldest operating electric tram network.
  • Cultural Institutions: The Indian Museum, established in 1814, is the oldest museum in India.
  • Sporting Passion: Football is a major part of Kolkata’s sports culture, with a fervent local fan base.
